How Sam Darnold turned around his career

seahawks

Author

Sam Darnold was the third overall draft pick in the 2018 NFL Draft by the New York Jets. A guy who had very high expectations coming out of the draft, being picked before guys like Josh Allen and Lamar Jackson. Unfortunately, Darnold did not live up to those expectations.

Three miserable years with the Jets, time with the Carolina Panthers that he would much rather forget about, and a backup role with the San Francisco 49ers in 2023 that he was handed. This had most NFL fans ready to give up entirely on the former third-overall draft pick. It couldn’t get any worse for Darnold, until Kevin O’Connell called and ultimately helped Darnold revitalize his career from being a guy written off to now a franchise quarterback for the Seattle Seahawks.

Code 
library(tidyverse)
library(ggrepel)
library(ggalt)


passing25 <- read_csv("2025NFLPassing.csv") |> mutate(Season = 2025, PlayerSeason = paste(Player, Season))

alltimepassing <- read_csv("nfl_qb_passing_19752025.csv")|> mutate(PlayerSeason = paste(Player, Season))


goodqb <- alltimepassing |>
  filter(PassingYds > 2500, TD >= 15)

career <- alltimepassing |> 
  group_by(Player) |> 
  arrange(Season) |> 
  mutate(
    CumulativePassingYards = cumsum(PassingYds)
  ) |>
  ungroup() |> 
  filter(Season > 2015) 

Darnold <- career |>
  filter(
    Player == "Sam Darnold")

Elite <- career |> 
  filter(Player == "Tom Brady" | Player == "Patrick Mahomes" | Player == "Cam Newton" | Player == "Andrew Luck" | Player == "Aaron Rodgers") 

elitelabel <- Elite |> 
  group_by(Player) |> 
  filter(Season == max(Season))

ggplot() + 
  geom_step(data=career, aes(x=Season, y=CumulativePassingYards, group=Player), color="grey", alpha=.4) +
  geom_step(data=Darnold, aes(x=Season, y=CumulativePassingYards, group=Player), color="#69BE28", linewidth=2) +
  geom_step(data=Elite, aes(x=Season, y=CumulativePassingYards, group=Player), color="red") +
  geom_point(data=elitelabel, aes(x=Season, y=CumulativePassingYards), color="red")+  
  geom_text_repel(data=elitelabel, aes(x=Season, y=CumulativePassingYards, label=Player))+
  scale_x_continuous(breaks = c(2016,2018,2020,2022,2024,2025), limits = c(2016,2025))+
  labs(
     title = "Sam Darnold is closing the gap in yards on NFL greats",
     subtitle = "From being benched to now currently being one of the leagues most prolific passers.",
     x = "",
     y = "Cumulative Passing Yards",
     caption = "Source: Pro Football Reference | By Ben Ofe"
     ) +
  theme_minimal() + 
  theme(
    plot.title = element_text(size = 14, face = "bold"),
    axis.title = element_text(size = 8), 
    plot.subtitle = element_text(size=10), 
    panel.grid.minor = element_blank(),
    plot.title.position = "plot"
    )

The cumulative yards is where the entire narrative of him restoring his career begins as a whole. You can see that his line was near the bottom of the NFL statistically while QB’s like Brady and Rodgers were at the top of the league in this category. There was a long stint in the line that it stayed almost nearly flat and didnt change much up until 2024. Where you can see Darnold’s line shoot straight up. This goes to show how Darndold in the last two years has made a huge jump and brought his career back on track.

As you can see for years here that Darnold was below the average QB rating while the other top NFL quarterbacks remained steady above the line and near the top. In his 2020 season with the New York Jets he reached a career low but has been trending upwards ever since then. He has recently surpassed the leagues averaged QB rating and improved vastly within the last few years. This goes to show lasting progress and that Sam Darnold wasn’t a one year wonder.

Code 
currentqb <- alltimepassing |>
  filter(PassingYds > 1000, TD >= 5, Season > 2020)

lowendqb <- currentqb |>
  filter(PassingYds > 2000, TD >= 20, Season > 2020)

midqb <- currentqb |>
  filter(PassingYds >= 3000, TD >= 30)

topqb <- currentqb |>
  filter(PassingYds >= 4500, TD >= 42)

qblabels <- bind_rows(topqb, Darnold)

ggplot() + 
  geom_point(data=currentqb, aes(x=`Cmp%`, y=`Y/G`), color="grey")+
  geom_point(data=lowendqb, aes(x=`Cmp%`, y=`Y/G`), color="grey")+
  geom_point(data=midqb, aes(x=`Cmp%`, y=`Y/G`), color="grey", alpha=.3)+
  geom_point(data=topqb, aes(x=`Cmp%`, y=`Y/G`), color="grey")+
  geom_point(data = Darnold,aes(x=`Cmp%`, y=`Y/G`), color="#69BE28")+
  geom_text_repel(data=qblabels, aes(x=`Cmp%`, y=`Y/G`, label=PlayerSeason))+
  labs(title="Darnold has flipped the script in the last two years", 
       subtitle="After years in the bottom half, Darnold's last two seasons put him among the most productive passers.",
       x="Completion Percentage",
       y="Yards per Game",
       caption = "Source: Pro Football Reference | By Ben Ofe") +
  theme_minimal() + 
  theme(
    plot.title = element_text(size = 14, face = "bold"),
    axis.title = element_text(size = 8), 
    plot.subtitle = element_text(size=10), 
    panel.grid.minor = element_blank(),
    plot.title.position = "plot"
    )

The scatterplot reflects how in the first six years of his career he was consistently in the bottom left of the graph due to poor play that led to a bad completion percentage and a low average yards per game. That right there tells the whole story of how he was struggling to be an efficient quarterback during that time. When in 2024 and 2025, his dots appear in the top right half of the graph. Sitting alongside names like Matthew Stafford and Patrick Mahomes who are seen as some of the top quarterbacks in the NFL.

I may be a little biased when it comes to it, but I think that Sam Darnold’s story is one of the better comeback stories to exist in the NFL. There was no reason for any coach or GM to believe in him after his first six years in the league putting up below average numbers with three different franchises but he bought in and proved the doubters wrong and that got him to where he is today with Mike Macdonald and the Seattle Seahawks. Where he just recently won a SuperBowl and has a three-year, $100 million contract.
